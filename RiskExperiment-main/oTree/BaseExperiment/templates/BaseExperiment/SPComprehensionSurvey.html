Sales performance in each region is the multiplication of the number of saleable sandwiches made and the selling price of each sandwich in that region.

5. What will be the payoffs of the regional managers?
They will receive half of the sales performance they achieve in each round.

6. What will be your payoff?
I will receive one fourth of the sales that each regional manager generates in each round.

{% endif%} 8. How do regional managers get to know about potential risks in their region?
Regional managers receive a local market survey report which informs them about potential risks (if any) that could impact their sales performance.

9. Do you have access to the local market survey report in each region?
No, I don't. Only the corresponding regional manager has access to the market report.

10. Do you have access to actual sales performance of regional managers?
Yes, I do.

11. What is your responsibility at the end of each round?
Allocating 10 minutes of production time between two regional managers for the next round, and deciding whether to spend the emergency monetary budget to deal with risks in each region for the next round.

12. What is the emergency monetary budget for?
The emergency monetary budget in each region can be used to reduce the impacts of potential risks on the sales performance.

13. Who can spend the emergency monetary budget?
Only I can spend this budget. The regional managers do not have access to this budget.

14. Is the use of the emergency monetary budget free of charge for you?
No, each time that I use this budget for a region, 10 cents will be deducted from my payoff and from the company's earnings.

15. How much time is each regional manager given to make sandwiches in the first round?
Five minutes

16. How much time is each regional manager given to make sandwiches in the next rounds?
This depends on my decision: for each round, I will distribute 10 minutes between the two regional managers.
